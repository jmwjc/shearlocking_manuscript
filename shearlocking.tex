\section{Optimal constraint ratio for shear locking}
The previous section has been mentioned that the shear locking is consisted of two parts, shear force locking and shear strain locking.
The corresponding LBB conditions are listed as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Shear force locking LBB condition:
        \begin{equation}
        \inf_{w_h\in W_h} \sup_{\boldsymbol q_h \in Q_h} \frac{\vert b_w(w_h,\boldsymbol q_h)\vert}{\Vert w_h\Vert_W \Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q} \ge \beta_q
        \end{equation}
    \item Shear strain locking LBB condition:
        \begin{equation}
        \inf_{\boldsymbol q_h \in Q_h} \sup_{\boldsymbol u_h \in W_h \times \Phi_h}
        \frac{\vert b(\boldsymbol q_h, \boldsymbol u_h) \vert}{\Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q \Vert \boldsymbol u_h \Vert_{W\times \Phi}} \ge \beta_\gamma
        \end{equation}
\end{itemize}
where, $\beta_q$ and $\beta_\gamma$ are LBB coefficients. The norms in the above LBB conditions are defined as follows:
\begin{align}
\Vert w_h \Vert_W^2 &= \int_\Omega \delta w_h kGh  w_h d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q^2 &= 
\int_\Omega \frac{\delta \boldsymbol q_h \cdot \boldsymbol q_h}{kGt} d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol \varphi_h \Vert_\Phi^2 &= 
\int_\Omega \delta \boldsymbol \kappa_h : \frac{t^3}{12} \boldsymbol D : \boldsymbol \kappa_h d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol u_h \Vert_{W\times\Phi}^2 &= 
\Vert w_h \Vert_W^2 + \Vert \boldsymbol \varphi_h \Vert_\Phi^2
\end{align}
Next, we will focus on these two LBB conditions respectively and employ the complete-polynormial-based estimate method \cite{wu2026} to investigate the relationship between their LBB coefficients $\beta_q$ and $\beta_\gamma$ and the degree of freedoms (DOFs) for variables $w_h$, $\boldsymbol \varphi_h$ and $\boldsymbol q_h$.
The detailed path for the complete-polynormial-based estimate method is lised in \ref{sec:estimator}.
Consequently, the optimal ratio of degrees of freedoms (DOFs) between variables can be revealed based on these two estimators.
