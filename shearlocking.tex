\section{Optimal constraint ratio for shear locking}
The previous section has been mentioned that the shear locking is consisted of two parts, shear force locking and shear strain locking.
The corresponding LBB conditions are listed as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Shear force locking LBB condition:
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:LBB_q}
        \inf_{w_h\in W_h} \sup_{\boldsymbol q_h \in Q_h} \frac{\vert b_w(w_h,\boldsymbol q_h)\vert}{\Vert w_h\Vert_W \Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q} \ge \beta_q
        \end{equation}
    \item Shear strain locking LBB condition:
        \begin{equation}\label{eq:LBB_gamma}
        \inf_{\boldsymbol q_h \in Q_h} \sup_{\boldsymbol u_h \in W_h \times \Phi_h}
        \frac{\vert b(\boldsymbol q_h, \boldsymbol u_h) \vert}{\Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q \Vert \boldsymbol u_h \Vert_{W\times \Phi}} \ge \beta_\gamma
        \end{equation}
\end{itemize}
where, $\beta_q$ and $\beta_\gamma$ are LBB coefficients. The norms in the above LBB conditions are defined as follows:
\begin{align}
\Vert w_h \Vert_W^2 &= \int_\Omega \delta w_h kGh  w_h d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol q_h \Vert_Q^2 &= 
\int_\Omega \frac{\delta \boldsymbol q_h \cdot \boldsymbol q_h}{kGt} d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol \varphi_h \Vert_\Phi^2 &= 
\int_\Omega \delta \boldsymbol \kappa_h : \frac{t^3}{12} \boldsymbol D : \boldsymbol \kappa_h d\Omega \\
\Vert \boldsymbol u_h \Vert_{W\times\Phi}^2 &= 
\Vert w_h \Vert_W^2 + \Vert \boldsymbol \varphi_h \Vert_\Phi^2
\end{align}
Next, we will focus on these two LBB conditions respectively and employ the complete-polynormial-based estimate method \cite{wu2026} to investigate the relationship between their LBB coefficients $\beta_q$ and $\beta_\gamma$ and the degree of freedoms (DOFs) for variables $w_h$, $\boldsymbol \varphi_h$ and $\boldsymbol q_h$.
The detailed path for the complete-polynormial-based estimate method is lised in \ref{sec:estimator}.
Consequently, the optimal ratio of degrees of freedoms (DOFs) between variables can be revealed based on these two estimators.

\subsection{Estimator for $\beta_q$}
The coefficient $\beta$ controls the behavior of shear force locking in Eq. \eqref{eq:force_locking_weak}, the detailed enforcement is listed as follows:
\begin{equation}
    \begin{aligned}
        &b_w(\delta w_h, \boldsymbol q_h) - f_w(\delta w_h) = (\delta w_h, \mathcal W \boldsymbol q_h) - f_w(\delta w_h) \\
        =& \int_{\Gamma_q} \delta w_h(\boldsymbol q_h \cdot \boldsymbol n - \bar q_n) d\Gamma - \int_\Omega \delta w_h (\nabla \cdot \boldsymbol q_h + \bar p) d\Omega \\
        =& 0
    \end{aligned}
\end{equation}
where, the constraints includes two parts, one is the equilibrium equation between shear force resultant and  transverse load in $\Omega$, the other is the natrual shear force boundary condition on $\Gamma_q$.
In accordance with the general form of completed-polynormial-based estimator, as shown in \ref{sec:estimator}, 
the operator $\mathcal W: Q\rightarrow W$ is a specific instance of $\mathcal P$ in Theorem.\ref{thm:estimator}, and is defined as:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal W = \left \{
    \begin{aligned}
        &\mathcal W^\Omega = \nabla \cdot & & \textrm{in}\; \Omega \\
        &\mathcal W^{\Gamma_q}\mathcal T^{\Gamma_q}, \; \mathcal W^{\Gamma_q} = \boldsymbol n \cdot & & \textrm{on}\; \Gamma_q
    \end{aligned}
    \right .
\end{equation}
where, based on the trace theorem \cite{}, $\mathcal T^{\Gamma_q}: Q(\Omega) \rightarrow Q(\Gamma_q)$ is a trace operator mapping the shear force $\boldsymbol q$ from domain $\Omega$ to the boundary $\Gamma_q$, while the parametric variable of $\boldsymbol q$ on boundary can be represented using $s$, i.e. $\boldsymbol q(s) \in Q(\Gamma_q)$. 
According to Theorem.\ref{thm:estimator}, the completed-polynomial-based estimator of $\beta_q$ has the following form:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:beta_q}
\beta_q \le \inf_{Q'\subset Q_{n_q} \setminus \ker \mathcal W_h \mathcal I_h^q}
\sup_{\boldsymbol q \in Q'}
\frac{\Vert \mathcal W\boldsymbol q \Vert_W}{\Vert \boldsymbol q \Vert_Q} + O(h)
\end{equation}
in which $\mathcal I_h^q:Q\rightarrow Q_h$ is the interpolation operator for shear force resultant.
$\mathcal W_h:Q_h\rightarrow W_h$ is the orthogonal operator of $\mathcal W$, i.e. $(\delta w_h, \mathcal W \boldsymbol q_h - \mathcal W_h \boldsymbol q_h)=0$.
$Q_{n_q}\subset Q(\Omega)$ is the completed polynomial space with the identical dimensions with $Q_h$, i.e. $\dim Q_{n_q} = \dim Q_h = 2n_q$.

From Eq. \eqref{eq:beta_q}, the LBB condition of Eq. \eqref{eq:LBB_q} can be maintained while the first term on right-hand side of Eq. \eqref{eq:beta_q} is non-zero, which should be necessarily required that:
\begin{equation}
    \dim \left(Q_{n_q} \setminus \ker \mathcal W_h \mathcal I_h^q\right) \le \dim \left(Q_{n_q} \setminus \ker \mathcal W\right)
\end{equation}
% where $\mathcal W\vert_{Q_{q_n}}$ is the restriction of $\mathcal W$ to space $Q_{q_n}$.
For mixed--formulation, the dimension of $Q_{n_q}\setminus \ker \mathcal W_h \mathcal I_h^q$ is evaluated by the dimension of $\mathrm{Im} \mathcal W_h$ and $\dim (\mathrm{Im} \mathcal W_h) = n_w$, yields:
\begin{equation}
    n_w = \dim \left(Q_{n_q} \setminus \ker \mathcal W_h \mathcal I_h^q\right)
\end{equation}

In contrast,
\begin{equation}
    % \begin{split}
